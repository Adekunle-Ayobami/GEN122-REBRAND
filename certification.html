Certified Nursing Assitant Training

A Certified Nursing Assistant assists patients with daily living and provides basic care. Most CNAs work in nursing homes, home health care services, and hospitals. They work under the supervision of licensed nursing staff, like registered nurses (RNs) or licensed practical nurses (LPNs). In many cases, the CNA serves as a patient's main caregiver. You may also refer to CNAs as:

  • Nursing assistants
  • Patient care assistants (PCAs)
  • Nurse aids
At GEN122, the training to become a certified nursing assitant is designed to provide individuals with the necessary qualifications and certification as professionals in the Global health and social industry.

FAQs

What does a Certified Nursing Assistant do?
As a certified nursing assistant, you'll perform a wide variety of tasks, from measuring a patient's daily caregiver within a nursing or residential home, and you'll often be your patients's main contact throughout the day. Depending on state and institutional regulations, you mya be asked to take on additional responsibilities, like administering medicine. Working as a certified nursing assistant can be deeply emotional and rewarding job since you're in constant interaction with your patients and you're directly impacting their quality of life.
How do you become a Certified Nursing Assistant?
There are many possible ways to go from being a Care Assistant to being a Nursing Assistant. The first step is by studying or taking a training program, run by an approved institution . Depending on your education and work experience, you may be able to earn your degree faster. Secondly, have a practical/hands-on session to familiarize yourself to what has been learnt.
What are the Skills needed?

The skills need to be a Certfied Nursing assistant can be divided into two categories:
Hard skills & Soft skills.

Hard skills are the technical skills and knowledge that CNAs need to perform their job duties safely and effectively. These skills include:

  • Basic Anatomy and Physiology
  • Medical Terminology
  • Patient care skilss, such as bathing, dressing, and feeding.

Soft skills are the interpersonal and communication skills that CNAs need to interact effectively with patients, families, and other healthcare professional. These skills include:

  • Compassion and Empathy
  • Patience
  • Communication Skills
Where do CNAs work?

The common CNA workplaces include:

  • Hospitals (state and local)
  • Skilled nursing facilities
  • Long-term residential facilities
  • Rehabilitation centre
  • Adult day care centres
